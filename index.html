10 Photography tips for begginers

Dec 01, 2017 By Noor
  1. Master Your Camera: Learn the basics of your camera's settings, including aperture, shutter speed, and ISO, to have creative control.
  2. Use Natural Light: Start with natural light; it's a beginner's best friend for soft, flattering illumination.
  3. Rule of Thirds: Compose your shots by dividing the frame into thirds both horizontally and vertically, placing key subjects at these intersections.
  4. Steady Your Shots: Invest in a tripod or steady your hands to eliminate camera shake and capture sharp images.
  5. Experiment and Practice: Don't be afraid to experiment with different angles, perspectives, and subjects. Practice regularly to improve your skills.
    6. ...
  6. Focus on Composition: Pay attention to framing, leading lines, and balance to create visually pleasing photos.
  7. Understand Depth of Field: Control depth of field by adjusting your aperture to blur backgrounds or keep everything sharp.
  8. Shoot in RAW: If your camera allows, shoot in RAW format for better post-processing flexibility.
  9. Learn Basic Editing: Familiarize yourself with basic photo editing tools to enhance your images.
  10. Study and Seek Feedback: Analyze the work of experienced photographers, join photography communities, and seek constructive feedback to continually grow as a photographer.

Perfect light for street art

Dec 01, 2017 By James Smith

The perfect light for street art is the golden hour, casting a warm and enchanting glow that accentuates colors and textures. This fleeting moment during sunrise or sunset bathes murals and graffiti in a soft, flattering light, adding depth and dimension to urban artworks. Shadows and highlights play harmoniously, enhancing the art's visual impact. Street artists often plan their work around this magical time to showcase their creations at their absolute best. When it comes to street art, timing truly is everything.
